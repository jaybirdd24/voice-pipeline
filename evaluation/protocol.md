# User Evaluation Protocol

## Research Question

Can a personalised TTS voice, trained from recordings of a parent or caregiver, be integrated
into a multilingual storytelling device — and how does it affect perceived engagement, perceived
understanding, and user preference compared with a generic TTS voice?

## Conditions

| Condition | Voice | Description |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| A (Personalised) | Piper fine-tuned on caregiver voice | The voice-pipeline output |
| B (Generic) | Device default TTS | iOS/Android/Pi built-in voice |

## Stimuli

Short bilingual story passages (~3–5 sentences each) read aloud in both conditions.
Each participant hears the same text in both voices (counterbalanced order).

Language coverage: English (en-AU), Mandarin (zh), Cantonese (yue).

Story texts: see `stories/` directory.

## Measures (7-point Likert scale, 1 = strongly disagree, 7 = strongly agree)

### Perceived Engagement
1. The voice held my attention.
2. I wanted to keep listening.
3. The voice felt expressive and lively.

### Perceived Understanding
4. I could understand the voice clearly.
5. The speech sounded natural.
6. The pronunciation was easy to follow.

### User Preference
7. I prefer this voice over the other.
8. I would want this voice to read stories to my child.

### Open response
- What did you like or dislike about each voice?

## Procedure

1. Participant signs consent form.
2. Brief introduction: "You will hear two short story passages read aloud. Please rate each one."
3. Play Condition A → participant completes ratings.
4. Short break.
5. Play Condition B → participant completes ratings.
6. Final preference question and open response.

Order of conditions is counterbalanced across participants.

## Sample Size Target

N = 10–15 participants (preliminary evaluation scope given timeline).

## Analysis

- Paired t-test / Wilcoxon signed-rank per measure (A vs B).
- Report effect size (Cohen's d) and p-values.
- Qualitative themes from open responses.

## Files

- `stimuli/` — audio files for each condition (generated by run_pipeline.sh)
- `data/` — anonymised response CSV (not committed to git)
- `results/` — analysis scripts and summary
